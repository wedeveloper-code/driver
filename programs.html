Course Curriculum

Our program meets all FMCSA Entry-Level Driver Training (ELDT) requirements. The 160-hour curriculum is divided into intensive classroom instruction, range practice, and real-world road driving.

Classroom Theory

  • Orientation & Safety Regulations
  • Logbooks & Hours of Service
  • Trip Planning & Map Reading
  • Accident Procedures & Prevention

Range & Road (Practical)

  • Pre-Trip, En-Route, & Post-Trip Inspections
  • Basic Control Skills (Backing, Parking)
  • Shifting & Double Clutching
  • City, Highway, & Rural Driving

Admissions Requirements

To enroll in the CDL Class A program, applicants must meet the following criteria before the first day of class:

  • Minimum 18 years of age (21 for interstate)
  • Valid state driver's license
  • Acceptable driving record (MVR)
  • Pass DOT Physical Exam
  • Pass DOT Drug Screen
  • CDL Learner's Permit (we help with this)

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get my CDL?
Our full-time program takes exactly 4 weeks (160 hours) to complete. Classes run Monday through Friday.
Do I need my Learner's Permit before starting?
While it is highly recommended to have your permit before class starts, our first week covers classroom theory which will prepare you to pass the written permit test at the DMV.
Is financial aid available?
Yes! We accept WIOA grants (which can cover 100% of tuition for eligible students), GI Bill® benefits for veterans, and offer flexible in-house payment plans.
Do you provide the truck for the DMV test?
Absolutely. You will take your final CDL skills test in the same truck you've been practicing in throughout the course, ensuring you are completely comfortable on test day.
